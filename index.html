Get your RPA operator's certificate

Once you have your licence, you may operate commercially with an operator (a holder of an RPA operators certificate, or ReOC), or apply for your own certificate.

To gain a ReOC you must complete an online application which involves:

  1. providing relevant details of your operation, drones and personnel
  2. pay the assessment fee (typically $950)
  3. participate in an interview with CASA.

You will be issued with a certificate upon successful completion.

Begin online ReOC application

Categories of operations

We issue certificates in the following categories:

  • multi-rotor
  • aeroplane
  • helicopter
  • powered lift
  • airship.

Your certificate will also contain a weight category. This will generally be up to 25kg (where required with 7kg restriction). If your RPA weighs more than 25kg, your certificate will record the manufacturer type and weight. Airships will be listed on your certificate detailing cubic capacity.

Renewing your operators certificate

Your initial ReOC certificate is valid for 12 months. We will send you a renewal notice for your initial certificate about three months before it is due to expire. This will include a renewal application and details of the fee payable.

Once the completed application form and renewal fee is received we will review the information. If satisfactory, we will issue you with a renewed certificate.

Additional authorisations such as permission, area approvals, or approvals that expire in line with your ReOC expiry will not be automatically renewed. Complete Form 11-09 and email it to rpas@casa.gov.au to reapply for a new permission.
